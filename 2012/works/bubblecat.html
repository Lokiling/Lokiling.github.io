Keywords

Interaction design, wireframe, web game design, team work, data analysis, user experience, logic design

Overview

After a market research, our team found that Casual Games on facebook and other SNS, like Bejeweled and Triple Town, are more and more popular. They have a large number of active users and their revenues are also surprisingly high. So we decided to make a new Casual Game, Bubble Cat, for Chinese users.

Players may be familiar with how to play Bubble Cat, because it likes Puzzle Bobble, a very popular and famous game. We started this project in early March, 2012. Our team was small, just included 3 programmers, 3 art designers, 1 game designer(also the team leader) and me, a product manager. However, all of the programmers and designer are professional, so we could make quick decisions and fast iterative. The beta version of Bubble Cat was finished in the late of May, 2012. Yep, we only spent 3 months to fulfill this new game project! And now, Bubble Cat is one of the most famous games on QZone and Pengyou --- both of them are Tencent's social networks, and has more than 3 million DAU(Daily Active Users) in China now.

My Role

As the product manager of Bubble Cat, I learned a lot from this project. This is the first game project that I participated from the beginning, so that setting up the rules of interfaces and interactions are not easy for me. Discussing with other professional product managers and reading many books of user experience and interaction design, I finished them subsequently.

All of the basic UI was designed by myself. My main tasks during this project were sketching, prototype design: wireframe designs and flowchart designs, copywriting, some of logical designs, interaction designs and communicating and discussing with programmers and art designer to make sure their works were following the initial design. Moreover, I need to use the analysis of users' data to improve my design and make sure all of UIs are user friendly.
