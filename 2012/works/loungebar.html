Keywords

Interaction design, wireframe, web game design, team work, data analysis, user experience, logic design

Overview

I got an intern job in Happy Elements Dec. 2010, and Lounge Bar was the first team I participated in. Lounge Bar is a typical SIM game: players own a small bar at the start. They can decorate their bar and learn new recipes of cocktails and desserts to attract more and more customers and earn more and more money.

Lounge Bar is on Mixi, a primary social network of Japan, facebook and Kaixin. It started operation in Feb. 2009. As a 1-year-old game, Lounge Bar had some technical issues and lots of brilliant ideas were confined. So we must designed new cool features and must be sure that it was feasible in programming. Our team was not so large, so all of members could sit down and had a brainstorm. Working with them, who are energetic and cooperative, I not only learned how to design a feature and wireframe, but also learned team spirit and patience.

My Role

At the beginning of my internship, I needed to adapt to work with a team. It's different from those projects I had done by myself during college. In a team, everyone has his / her own responsibility. As a product manager, most of my works needed to communicate with others, like telling programmers what was the feature, how to use it and what is the workflow, discussing with art designers how to make a UI more friendly and comfortable.

I designed two extremely successful features of this game: Decoration Upgrading System (you will see one of my sketches of this system above) and Special Guest. The former one continuously improve Lounge Bar's revenue on Mixi, easy to reuse; and the later one reversed the decline of Lounger Bar's DAU (Daily Active User).

Thanks to Lounge Bar Team, I thought I grew a lot. I know how to make a user friendly UI, how to use relevant software, such as Axure, Visio, Balsamiq, etc. how to analysis data and, more importantly, how to work within a team.
